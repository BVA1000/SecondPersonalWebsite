My Story

Originally I was born in a place called Riga, Latvia, located in North Eastern Europe. I enjoyed my childhood and got to experience the European lifestyle before moving to the Unted States. I moved to California in 2009 because most of my family lives in the United States. I was also born as a U.S. citizen abroad so it was easier for me to get settled.

I have lived in California for twelve years. During my time there I graduated from Shasta High School and my favorite thing about it during my attendance was being part of the swim team. I started as a Junior Varsity swimmer as a Freshman to making it to Masters as a Senior. I love the sport and is a great exercise for the body. Then I went to attend Shasta College for two and a half years.

During my first and second semesters in Shasta College, I heard about the Bethel Tech program and was considering the idea of becoming a Full Stack Developer. At that time I knew nothing about coding, but I always found technology, particularly apps to be interesting, and how someone would go about creating them. So, during my second semester in Shasta College, I took a course learning Java, but it did not go as well as I hoped. In the end, I failed the class because it was hard for me and I did not know how to ask the right questions at the time. I also realized because my teacher only had knowldge on Windows and Microsoft devices he could not help me because I used Apple. Parts of it were going well for me, but in the end as it got more complex and I had to download certain applications that were not available for the Mac platform, I got frustrated and decided not to take the final. After my experience I left the idea of becoming a developer on the back burner for almost two years.

Three semesters go by and I still did not know what I wanted to do with my life. In the middle of my fifth semester, I heard about how Bethel Tech was growing and how students were able to find opportunities in the workspace. I decided to give it a second thought. Near the end of my fifth semester, I was doing my own research on Full Stack Web Development again, and this time looked at the types of technologies and languages that Bethel Tech had to offer in their program. After doing my research and signing up for a three month short course that would teach me the basics of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, I decided to stop going to Shasta College and focus on this short course and work two jobs which would lead me to creating my savings for Bethel Tech.

In the beginning of 2020, I took the short introductory course on how to build a Full Stack Application from start to finish. The course was being taught by an actual developer with fourteen years of experience and he worked on cool projects like building programs for the Navy's nuclear submarines. I really liked the course and learned more about the process of prgramming an application from start to finish in a short three month course than I did in college! I was learning HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Ruby On Rails, AWS, and Heroku. With these technologies, I built a social media application where users can write and send posts, look throught posts, login and logout, and create or delete a user account. After my experience in the short course, I decided to work between 55 to 65 hours a week till next year in February to save for the Bethel Tech Full Stack program.

I acheived my goal of saving up for the program and started February 15th. I am currently in the program and set to graduate in late October of this year. It is a part time program which lasts for about nine months. I have more information on what I have learned in the program so far in my projects section which you can access here. Overall I have enjoyed my experience in Bethel Tech so far from learning the different types of technologies for building a Full Stack application from the front to back end to getting to know like-minded people online who have similar ambitions.
